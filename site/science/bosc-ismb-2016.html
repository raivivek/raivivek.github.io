Overview of BOSC and ISMB, 2016

This year I went to attend the Intelligent Systems for Molecular Biology (ISMB) from July 8-12 in Orlando, Florida. But before I talk about my experience of the conference (and amazing Disney World), let me roll back a bit and tell you about why I was there.

Several months ago, I wrote a [short] () describing my submission for ISCB Wikipedia article writing competition. To recall, the competition invited people to improve upon existing computational biology resources on Wikipedia by writing articles, expanding and correcting previous information, adding metadata and so on. The aim is simple -- to propagate the idea of open-science and open-research, and to propagate the collective community efforts to pursue science and curate knowledge. So, after the official editing period finished in January, I got busy in other college activities and nearly forgot about it until suddenly I received an email from Alex Bateman in June. He is an active Wikipedian and also one of the organizers of the event. Long story short, I had won the third place in Wikipedia category and first place in Wikidata category. So, he invited me to come over to ISMB and receive the award in person if possible.
