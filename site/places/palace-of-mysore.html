The Palace of Mysore

1 day. 6 people. 3 places.

Winter school was on in full swing with intensive pre-school and serious discussions that often spilled over to the relatively relaxed moments of intermittent breaks. Everyone was craving for a day break. Participants not from India desperately wanted to shop around the city and see new places, and regulars just wanted to retire to their homes and enjoy a jolly weekend.

By Saturday night, almost everyone had deserted the campus (except those who explicitly didn't want to). I don't know how it happened (thanks to Sanjay Lamba), but six of us got together and agreed upon a Mysore rendezvous.

That journey turned out to be one of my most momentous journeys so far!

We set out at 4 am in the morning from ICTS campus. I didn't sleep the night before hoping that I'd get a sleep in the car instead. Unfortunately, my wishes were granted half-heartedly as I could sleep only for an hour or two.

Passing Mysore

Mysore (now called Mysuru) has a delightful skyline! The background is consistently spotted with hills, trying to shade the city from bright rays of sun. Occasionally, these hills would have a temple or fort at their summits, although appearing to me as miniature models from the distance.

Read interesting trivia about Mysore.


On the way, we decided to skip Mysuru for now and move ahead to Bandipur National Park and Tiger Reserve. It was roughly an hour of drive from Mysore.

Bandipur National Park

We reached Bandipur National Park at around 9.15 am. Interestingly, two national highways are built through the tiger reserve, allowing one to pass along stretches where one could easily spot an elephant or deer. Driving slowly, we reached the official army station and parked the car. Suddenly, we learned that last safari ride for the jungle was leaving in 5 minutes. It was going to be my first safari ride of life!
